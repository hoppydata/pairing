# 40K Pairing Rules (5-Player)

## Before You Begin

Captains **roll off** to determine which team chooses its table first. That team gains the **Table Choice Token** and is designated **Team A**. The other team is **Team B**.

> **The Matrix:** Before the round starts, have two questions answered for every army in your roster: *What does this army most want to play against?* and *What most wants to play against this army?* These answers drive every decision below. See the [Pairing Strategy Guide](data/pairing-strategy.md#the-matrix).

---

## Stage 1

### 1. Defender Reveal

Team A and Team B secretly choose their **first defender** and reveal simultaneously.

> **Strategy — Avoiding a Red:** If a player has one red matchup to avoid, defending here guarantees they won't face it in Stage 1. Attacking instead risks being forced into it later. See [Avoiding a Red](data/pairing-strategy.md#avoiding-a-red) for full odd/even team variants.

### 2. Attacker Selection

Team A and Team B each secretly choose **two attackers** to send against the opposing defender, then reveal simultaneously.

At this point, two players remain in each team's pool.

> **Strategy — Take Out the Trash:** If you have multiple lists that are weak into the same army, prioritise getting them off the board now rather than letting them pile up in the scrum. Accepting a suboptimal matchup here can protect your matrix later. See [Take Out the Trash](data/pairing-strategy.md#take-out-the-trash).

### 3. Refusal

- Team A takes the two attackers offered by Team B and secretly chooses **one to refuse**.
- Team B takes the two attackers offered by Team A and secretly chooses **one to refuse**.
- Refused attackers are revealed simultaneously.
- Refused attackers return to their team's pool.

### 4. Result

Two pairings are now determined. Each team has **three players** remaining in its pool.

### 5. Table Pick (Stage 1)

Starting with **Team A**, the two defenders pick their tables.
After both picks, **pass the Table Choice Token to Team B**.

---

## Stage 2

### 1. Defender Reveal

Team A and Team B secretly choose their **second defender** and reveal simultaneously.

> **Strategy — Avoiding a Red:** Same principle as Stage 1. In an even-player team running a scrum, there is also the option of saving the red player for the forgotten slot — defend with something that *can* play the red, then attack into every other defender. See [Avoiding a Red](data/pairing-strategy.md#avoiding-a-red) for the full even-team scrum variant.

### 2. Attacker Selection

Team A and Team B each secretly choose **two attackers** to send against the opposing defender, then reveal simultaneously.

> **Strategy — Take Out the Trash:** This is the last opportunity to manage a weak scrum matrix. If problematic lists are still in the pool, consider using attacker selection to remove them now rather than carrying a bad matrix into the refused/forgotten pairings.

> **Strategy — Pin:** If you are executing a pin, hold your two pin counters as the last two attackers you send. The goal is to give the opponent's defender two bad matchups to choose between. In an even-player team, note that the pin target may be hidden as the forgotten card — you need a third counter to guarantee the pin in that case. See [Pin](data/pairing-strategy.md#pin).

### 3. Refusal

- Team A takes the two attackers offered by Team B and secretly chooses **one to refuse**.
- Team B takes the two attackers offered by Team A and secretly chooses **one to refuse**.
- Refused attackers are revealed simultaneously.
- The refused attackers are **automatically paired against each other** and designated to play **Game 5**.

> **Strategy — Pin (execution):** If the pin target is also attacking, refuse it here so it falls into the Game 5 refused pair against one of your pin counters.

### 4. Table Pick (Stage 2)

Starting with **Team B**, defenders pick their tables. The **last remaining table** is assigned to the Game 5 pairing (the two refused attackers).
